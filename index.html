About

My name is Katy Gimma. I grew up in a small town of 800 people in New York State and spent my time exploring the woods and local wildlife with my friends.

This interest in the world around me lead me to complete a bachelor’s degree in Biology from the University of Plattsburgh. My focus was in Ethnobotany, the relationship between people and plants. I loved finding interesting ways to convey the relationships between people and plants in a way that inspired my peers to develop personal connections with medicinal plants. Since graduating from college, I have worn many hats.

First, I was the activities coordinator for refugee and at-risk youth in Burlington, Vermont. I worked with teens and their families. We sought out the best opportunities for each family and developed ways to advocate for their needs. We collaborated on ways to preserve their own culture while navigating a new one.

Then, I worked as the team leader for the inaugural year of ECO AmeriCorps in the Department of Environmental Conservation (DEC) in Vermont. I lead a team of 20 other AmeriCorps members running conservation, restoration, and waste management programs around the state. I helped the team develop the skills to carry out scientific investigations, then communicate the findings to non-professionals such as community members, students, and politicians.

My most recent role has been in education. I was a seventh-grade science teacher in my hometown. Although hesitant at first, this was one of my favorite jobs. As has been the theme of my life, my job was to take complex scientific knowledge and convert it to something entertaining, useful and understandable in the mind of a twelve-year-old.

After teaching in the middle school, I wanted to travel, but I wasn’t ready to give up my role as an educator. I started teaching English virtually. I have been teaching English online for two years, now. In this position I still have the opportunity to find new ways to communicate patterns and build an understanding of something new and difficult to students from around the world.

I recently completed a data analyst boot camp through Thinkful Academy. In my time as a student I have become professionally proficient in Excel, SQL, Python + pandas, and Tableau. I have learned how to utilize these applications to investigate and effectively communicate statistically significant patterns and changes for businesses, bitcoin data, and national level peace initiatives.

I am excited to be able to combine my love for communicating complex ideas with my newly developed skills in web scraping and data analysis.
